<details>
  <summary>Notes on the J4+J5+J6+J7 to Hub Cable.</summary>

  ### Summary

  There is an interference issue with the connector, and customization is required for cables purchased from LCSC.

  This issue does not occur with the multiple manufacturers producing OpenArm units.

  ### L-Shaped Connector (Enactic In-house Method)

  It needs to be modified if the cable was purchased from LCSC.

  Process the custom L-shaped connector as indicated by the blue circles in the images below.

  #### Processing Steps

  3D Data: [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/u/0/folders/11rchLwVdV34ebEQL1X1ZmB4Xrl4hs9kZ)

  * mold: `cables_L-shapedMold.step`
  * black case: `cables_L-shapedCase.step`

  #### 1. Prepare the mold and the black case, then insert the black case into the mold.

  <table>
    <tr style={{ border: "none" }}>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step1-1.jpg"

                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step1-2"
                    width="50%" />
      </td>
    </tr>
  </table>

  #### 2. Place the mold with the recessed side facing upward, and insert the terminal that will be bent into an L shape.

  :::note
  Ensure the thin cable coming out of the terminal is on the **right side**.
  :::

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step2.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step2" width="30%" />

  #### 3. Firmly bend the cable until it lies flat.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step3.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step3" width="30%" />

  #### 4. Prepare the mold cover and place it on top.

  <table>
    <tr style={{ border: "none" }}>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step4-1.jpg"
                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step4-1"
                    width="80%" />
      </td>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step4-2.jpg"
                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step4-2"
                    width="80%" />
      </td>
    </tr>
  </table>

  #### 5. Secure the four corners with nuts and screws.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step5.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step5" width="30%" />

  #### 6. Turn the mold over.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step6.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step6" width="30%" />

  #### 7. Melt the hot glue stick and inject it into the **outer holes**.

  :::note
  Do not pull the trigger. Instead, press the glue stick itself firmly to melt and push it in.
  :::

  <table>
    <tr style={{ border: "none" }}>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "centor", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step7-1.jpg"

                    width="80%" />
      </td>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>

                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step7-2"
                    width="70%" />
      </td>
    </tr>
  </table>

  #### 8. When the melted glue comes out from the middle hole as shown below, the process is complete.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step8.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step8" width="30%" />

  #### 9. Wait about two minutes, then cut off the hardened glue with nippers.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step9.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step9" width="30%" />

  #### 10. Insert a flathead screwdriver into the mold gap and remove the cover.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step10.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step10" width="30%" />

  #### 11. After removing the cover, cut off the two protruding parts of the hardened glue with nippers.

  <table>
    <tr style={{ border: "none" }}>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step11-1.jpg"
                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step11-1"
                    width="70%" />
      </td>
      <td style={{ width: "50%", verticalAlign: "top", border: "none" }}>
        <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step11-2.jpg"
                    alt="Enactic In-house Method step11-2"
                    width="70%" />
      </td>
    </tr>
  </table>

  #### 12. Use a flathead screwdriver to remove the black case on the back side.

  <BlockImage src="hardware/wiring-and-casing-guide/supplementary-information/enactic-method-step12.jpg" alt="Enactic In-house Method step12" width="30%" />

  #### 13. From the opened black case side, push the terminal with your finger to remove it from the mold.
